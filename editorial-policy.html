Editorial policy

How EnglishArena content is prepared and corrected

This policy explains the standards used for lessons, question templates, updates, and reader corrections.

1. Original educational value

Lessons are written to explain rules in plain language, add examples, compare confusing forms, and identify common mistakes. We do not publish scraped articles or copy-pasted answer keys. General grammar facts are presented through original structure and wording.

2. Question design

Quiz items are produced from reviewed word entries, grammar patterns, and deterministic variations. Distractors should be plausible enough to test understanding without becoming ambiguous. Because generated patterns can repeat, the site describes the bank as practice combinations rather than pretending that every item is a separately authored question.

3. Review priorities

We check grammatical accuracy, answer uniqueness, explanation clarity, spelling, mobile display, keyboard accessibility, and broken links. High-impact issues—such as a wrong correct answer or an ambiguous question—receive priority over cosmetic edits.

4. Sources and judgment

English usage varies by region and context. When more than one form is accepted, lessons should acknowledge the variation rather than present one variety as universally correct. Official exam instructions and a learner’s prescribed textbook take priority for a specific examination.

5. Corrections

Readers can report an issue through the contact page. A useful report includes the page title or question ID, the exact wording, the suspected problem, and a suggested correction or reference. Confirmed errors are corrected in the source files and included in the next published version.

6. Advertising separation

Editorial decisions are not sold to advertisers. If advertising is added later, ads will be visually separated from lessons and quiz controls. The site will not ask readers to click advertisements or disguise ads as answers, navigation, or download buttons.
