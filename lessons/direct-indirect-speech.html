Direct and Indirect Speech

Direct speech repeats exact words inside quotation marks. Indirect speech reports the message and adjusts grammar to fit the new speaker, time, and situation.

1. Reporting statements

Direct: Rina said, “I am tired.” Indirect: Rina said that she was tired. When the reporting verb is in the past, the reported tense often shifts back: present simple to past simple, present continuous to past continuous, and present perfect to past perfect.

Backshift is not always necessary when the information is still true or when the reporting verb is present: “The teacher says that water boils at 100°C.”

2. Pronouns and time expressions

Pronouns change according to the speaker and listener. Time and place words may also change when the reporting context changes.

Direct formCommon reported form
todaythat day
tomorrowthe next day
yesterdaythe previous day
herethere
thisthat

Do not change these words mechanically if the place or time is still the same.

3. Reporting questions

Use statement word order in indirect questions. Yes/no questions usually take if or whether: “Are you ready?” becomes “She asked whether I was ready.” Wh-questions keep the question word: “Where do you live?” becomes “He asked where I lived.”

Do not use a question mark unless the whole reporting sentence is itself a question.

4. Commands and requests

Commands are commonly reported with tell + object + to-infinitive: “Close the door,” the teacher said to Ali → “The teacher told Ali to close the door.” Negative commands use not to. Requests commonly use ask + object + to-infinitive.

“Please wait here,” she said. → She asked me to wait there.

“Do not touch the wire,” he said. → He told us not to touch the wire.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Keeping question word order after asked.
  • Changing pronouns without considering who originally spoke and who is reporting.
  • Using said me; use said to me or told me.
Self-check

Test the key ideas

Report: He said, “I have finished.”

Answer: He said that he had finished.

Report: She asked, “Where are you going?”

Answer: She asked where I was going.

Report: The doctor said, “Take this medicine.”

Answer: The doctor told me to take that medicine.
