Active and Passive Voice

Voice changes the focus of a sentence. Active voice highlights the doer; passive voice highlights the action, result, or receiver.

1. Core structure

Active structure: subject + verb + object. Passive structure: object + a form of be + past participle, followed by by + agent only when the agent matters.

Active: The committee approved the proposal.

Passive: The proposal was approved by the committee.

The tense is carried by the form of be. The main verb becomes a past participle.

2. Common tense patterns

Active tensePassive patternExample
Present simpleam/is/are + past participleThe reports are checked.
Past simplewas/were + past participleThe reports were checked.
Present perfecthas/have been + past participleThe reports have been checked.
Future with willwill be + past participleThe reports will be checked.
Modalmodal + be + past participleThe reports must be checked.

3. When passive voice is useful

Use passive voice when the doer is unknown, obvious, unimportant, or deliberately backgrounded: “My bicycle was stolen”; “The samples were tested at room temperature.” It is also useful when the result deserves first position.

Passive voice is not automatically formal or better. In instructions and clear business writing, active voice is often shorter: “Send the document today.” Choose according to focus, not habit.

4. Verbs that cannot become passive

Only verbs that take an object can normally form a passive sentence. “The baby slept” has no object, so it cannot become passive. Some verbs take two objects and allow two passive forms: “They gave Mina a prize” can become “Mina was given a prize” or “A prize was given to Mina.”

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Changing the main tense while forming the passive.
  • Using the base verb instead of the past participle after be.
  • Keeping an unnecessary by-phrase: “The window was broken” is often enough.
Self-check

Test the key ideas

Change to passive: “They completed the bridge.”

Answer: The bridge was completed.

What carries tense in a passive structure?

Answer: The form of be

Can “The child laughed” normally be passive?

Answer: No, because it has no object.
