English MCQ Exam Strategies

Good exam technique cannot replace knowledge, but it helps you use what you know under time pressure. The goal is a consistent decision process, not lucky guessing.

1. Read the task before the sentence

Identify what the question is testing: grammar, meaning, error detection, completion, or spelling. This prevents you from solving the wrong problem. Underline signal words such as not, except, closest, and best.

2. Predict before checking options

Complete the sentence mentally before looking at the answers. Your prediction may not match word for word, but it creates a standard for evaluating options and reduces distraction from plausible wrong answers.

For vocabulary in context, decide whether the missing word should be positive or negative, formal or informal, and which part of speech is required.

3. Eliminate with evidence

Reject options for a stated reason: wrong tense, wrong agreement, impossible meaning, unsuitable preposition, or incorrect collocation. When two options remain, reread the entire sentence rather than comparing only the two words.

Useful question: “What exact rule or meaning makes this option better?” If you cannot answer, mark the item for review.

4. Manage time and review

Use a first pass for confident answers. Do not spend a large part of the test on one item. On the second pass, solve marked questions and check negatives, answer-sheet alignment, and unanswered items.

After practice, classify every mistake: knowledge gap, misread instruction, careless grammar, weak vocabulary, or time pressure. Your next study session should target the largest category.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Changing a correct answer without a concrete reason.
  • Spending equal time on easy and difficult questions.
  • Reviewing only the score instead of the cause of each error.
Self-check

Test the key ideas

What should you do before reading the options?

Answer: Predict the needed meaning or form.

What is a good reason to change an answer?

Answer: A specific rule or evidence, not nervousness.

How should mistakes be reviewed?

Answer: Classify their cause and target the biggest weakness.
